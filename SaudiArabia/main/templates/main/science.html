Scientific Saudi Achievements

Pioneering Visionary

Dr. Hayat Sindi

"My mission in life and contribute something to humanity. For me, science is a universal language that transcends nationality, religion, and gender."

Dr. Hayat Sindi is a pioneering Saudi biomedical scientist and social entrepreneur, celebrated for becoming the first Saudi woman to earn a Ph.D. in biotechnology from Cambridge University. Her defining achievement lies in her commitment to global health equity, leading the co-founding of Diagnostics for All (DFA), an initiative focused on creating simple, affordable diagnostic devices that can be used in low-resource settings. She also established the i2 (Institute for Imagination and Ingenuity) to foster a culture of science and entrepreneurship among youth in the Middle East. Recognized for her global influence, Dr. Sindi was appointed as a UNESCO Goodwill Ambassador, promoting science education, and made history as one of the first women appointed to the Saudi Shura Council, solidifying her role as a leader in scientific innovation and national policy.

Pioneering Visionary

Zainab Alturaiki

Zainab Alturaiki is celebrated as a pioneer in Saudi Arabia's technology education landscape, notably for being a member of the inaugural female cohort to be admitted to the prestigious King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals (KFUPM) for a Bachelor of Science in Software Engineering. Excelling in her studies, particularly in Artificial Intelligence (AI), she earned nomination to participate in the KAUST Academy AI Program and represent KFUPM in global programming competitions. Beyond her academic success, Alturaiki demonstrated key leadership by founding the KFUPM Female Computer Club, actively working to empower and support women in the tech sector and serving as a Global Engagement Ambassador for the university.
