Vision 2030

From resilience to innovation — Saudi Arabia’s journey toward a diversified, empowered, and globally competitive future.

Transformation Timeline

  1. 2016 — Launch of Vision 2030

    Vision 2030 announced to diversify the economy and reduce oil dependence — a roadmap led by H.R.H. Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman.

  2. 2017 — National Transformation Program

    Government agencies began modernization through the National Transformation Program — focusing on digital services, performance, and transparency.

  3. 2019 — Giga Projects Begin

    Launch of NEOM, The Red Sea, Qiddiya, and AMAALA — creating new economic and tourism sectors while attracting global investment.

  4. 2020 — Resilience During COVID-19

    The pandemic became a real-world test of Vision 2030, accelerating digital transformation and proving the nation’s unity and adaptability.

    • Nationwide remote learning via Madrasati and IEN platforms.
    • Health digitization with Tawakkalna and Sehhaty apps.
    • Expansion of e-government services and remote work systems.
    • Financial support programs for citizens and small businesses.
    • Advanced data systems enabled real-time national decisions.

    Source: Vision 2030 Reports (2020–2021), MCIT, MOH

  5. 2021–2023 — Global Recognition

    Saudi Arabia achieved record milestones in digital government, economic diversification, and social empowerment.

    • Women’s workforce participation exceeded Vision 2030 target, reaching 37% in 2022 (target was 30%).
    • Ranked among the top 10 globally in digital government maturity (UN e-Government Index 2022).
    • Hosting of global summits like LEAP and FII, reinforcing Saudi’s innovation leadership.

    Source: GASTAT, Vision 2030 Reports, UN e-Government Index

  6. 2030 — Vision Realized

    A thriving, diversified economy built on innovation, renewable energy, and human capital — a global model for sustainable growth.

Insight: Vision 2030 is not just a plan — it’s a mindset of continuous progress and resilience.
